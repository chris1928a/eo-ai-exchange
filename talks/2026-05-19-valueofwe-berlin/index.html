The famous one
ChatGPT
by OpenAI
  • Biggest user base, easiest to start
  • Voice mode and image generation
  • Widest plugin and GPT library
  • Strong at quick, single-shot answers
The Google one
Gemini
by Google
  • Native inside Google Workspace
  • YouTube and video understanding
  • Generous free tier
  • Largest context window in free tier
All three work. Pick the one your team’s tools live closest to. I picked Claude because the work I do (long client docs, multi-step research, careful drafting) is exactly what it’s best at.

How the twin is built

Memory. Tools. Workflows. Skills.
One front door.

Front door
One chat. It picks the right playbook and calls the right tools for the question you ask.
Skills
Mini playbooks, one per lane. Wedding decisions, property pricing, client briefs, legal filings. Each one is a paragraph of plain text.
Workflows
The recurring rituals. Morning brief. Meeting prep. Drafts. Friday review.
Tools
Things you already use every day. Email, calendar, drive, notes, contacts. Nothing new to install.
Memory
Nothing falls out. Decisions, meeting notes, contact context. All searchable, forever.
All four parts live in plain files. Portable. Yours. No vendor lock-in.

Inside the architecture, what’s a skill

A skill is a paragraph of plain text.
No code.

Example skill
/morning-brief
The recipe Claude reads every morning at 06:30. Tells it what to fetch, what I care about, and what to send back. Written once, in plain English.
I have 20+ of these, one per recurring task. When a task comes up, the right skill loads automatically.
# Skill: morning-brief load when: every weekday at 06:30 ## What to do 1. Read my unread inbox. 2. Read today’s calendar. 3. Pick the 3 priorities I’m at risk of missing. 4. Send me one page on Telegram. Under 200 words. ## What it knows about me My six lanes (client work, ventures, real estate, ...) My voice rules (plain text, no fluff) Last week’s open loops and decisions ## Output One Telegram message at 06:32. Three priorities. Three open threads. Four calls today.

Honest, where it still goes wrong

Four traps I fell into.
So you don’t.

01
Tools come and go
Apps disappear. Pricing flips overnight. Stay in plain files everyone can open. The workflow has to survive the tool.
02
AI makes things up
Models guess on long-tail data. Force citations. Never trust a number, name, or date without checking the source.
03
Client data has limits
Decide what AI is allowed to touch before you start, not after. Build the privacy fence first, the workflow second.
04
It’s people, not tech
7 out of 10 of these projects fail because no one uses them. Adoption is the moat, not the model.

What this means for an agency like yours

Don’t perfect the setup.
Ship the first workflow this week.

01
Find the drain
Which repetitive task burns more than 5h a week on your team? Client recap? Status update? Asset routing? Brief writing?
02
Wrap one workflow
Memory plus tools plus a short, plain-text instruction. Don’t build a platform. Build a habit.
03
One twin per role
An Account Manager twin. A Creative twin. An Ops twin. Don’t try to build one twin for everyone. Pick one role and ship.
04
Kill it if it doesn’t pay back in a week
If a workflow doesn’t save real time inside seven days, the workflow is wrong, not the tech. Try the next one.
