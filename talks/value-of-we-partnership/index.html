03 / 12
How it actually thinks

5 layers.
Every strategic decision runs through them, in order.

Layer 01
Sales + Delivery CRM Reality
Wons, losses, pipeline, customer concentration, active mandates. Hard facts before opinions.
Layer 02
Financial Dashboard
Cashflow, headcount, liquidity drawdown trend, KPIs. The decision has to survive the P&L.
Layer 03
Quarterly OKRs
Sprint commitments. A decision that breaks the sprint needs a louder reason.
Layer 04
Strategy Lock
ICP, positioning, pricing, service tiers, tagline. The frozen choices the company has already paid to make.
Layer 05
Mentor Avatars
Pattern-match against 3-5 of the best operators in your space. What would the smartest peer say to this?
A decision that contradicts 3 or more layers triggers a stop-and-think alert. The cockpit refuses to push it through.

What’s under the hood

All corporate data.
Pulled live. Always current.

Pull What it does Live numbers (RC, today)
close_bridge.py Sales-CRM: opportunities, wons, lost, pipeline, lead-pool, lost-reasons 411 opps, 80 wons, 530k EUR active pipeline, 20% qualified won-rate
crm_bridge.py Delivery-CRM: active mandates, companies, candidates, recruiter ownership 15 active mandates, 391 companies, 1,000+ candidates, 26 users
financial_pull.py Liquidity, headcount, sales/recruiting KPIs from the live finance sheet 152k EUR avg monthly revenue Q1, 130-210k personnel cost, drawdown tracked
pipeline_daily.py Daily snapshot + diff vs. yesterday + risk flags (stagnation, overdue, drops) Runs Mon-Fri 18:30 CET. Alerts on stage-stagnation >7 days, pipeline drop >10%
stress_detector.py Pre-decision stress score with 4 trigger categories. Refuses pushes when red. Score 0-100. Red = pause-template instead of decision-push
renewal_tracker.py Customer renewal-cycle from won-history + custom-field override. Heatmap. UPCOMING / RENEWAL_WINDOW / OVERDUE per customer, 60-day rolling view
All in Python. All in git. ~200 lines per pull. No vendor platform. No n8n, no make.com. Cron + Lambda for the schedules.
