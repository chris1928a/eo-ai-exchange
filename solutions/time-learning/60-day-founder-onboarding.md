# 60-Day Founder Onboarding

**Pain cluster:** Time / Learning (6 of 85, plus all 10 Beginners)
**Status:** Released, Event #1, 2026-05-11
**Time investment:** ~6 hours total over 60 days = 6 minutes per day average

> The brutally practical 60-day path from „I have no AI setup" to „I have ONE working AI workflow that saves me 4-8 hours per week". For founders who have 10 minutes per day, not 10 hours per week.

---

## Why this exists

10 Beginner-level audience members at Event #1 plus 6 explicit „time/learning" pain expressers: „Time to learn more", „Finding time to learn and implement ideas", „I don't yet have a basis to work from", „not to invest too much time in AI".

Every „learn AI" guide in 2026 either assumes you have 20 hours/week (Builder path) or 0 hours and somehow learn anyway (vague „just play with it"). Neither works for time-poor founders.

This is the in-between: 6 minutes per day for 60 days. By day 61 you have ONE workflow that returns 40-80x the time invested.

---

## The 2026 reality for beginners

- Anthropic launched a free learning platform in March 2026 with 13 self-paced courses + real certificates
- Two paths exist: Power User (no code, results in days) vs Builder (technical, months)
- Email triage is the universal starter use case (every founder has email pain)
- 80% of ROI comes from ONE working workflow, not from broad familiarity
- The biggest blocker is not AI difficulty, it is **time discipline**

This 60-day plan is the Power User path, optimized for time-discipline.

## The plan, week by week

### Week 1, Setup (60 min total, 10 min/day)

**Goal:** account + ONE use case picked + first prompt sent.

- **Day 1 (10 min):** Sign up for Claude Pro (claude.com/pricing, $20/month). Skip Free tier, the limits will frustrate you.
- **Day 2 (10 min):** Pick ONE use case from this list:
  1. Email triage (most common starter, recommended)
  2. Meeting summary
  3. Daily brief (calendar + tasks + priorities)
  4. Status report writing (weekly to team or board)
  5. Inbound message reply drafts (LinkedIn DMs, customer support)
- **Day 3-5 (10 min/day):** Use Claude Browser for that ONE use case. Just open Claude, paste your stuff, ask for help. No setup, no skills, no Code.
- **Day 6-7 (off):** Reflect. Did the use case feel useful? Did you actually use it 3+ times? If yes: continue. If no: switch use case before Week 2.

### Week 2-3, Use it daily (140 min total, 10 min/day)

**Goal:** the use case becomes muscle memory.

- **Daily ritual:** every morning, 10 min on your one use case
- **Friction log:** keep a 1-line note each day, „what slowed me down". Examples: „Pasting email is annoying", „Output too long", „Misses context about my company"
- **End of week 3:** review friction log. The patterns will tell you what to formalize next.

### Week 4-5, Formalize (100 min total)

**Goal:** turn ad-hoc usage into a repeatable system.

- **Day 22-25 (15 min/day):** create a CLAUDE.md template (see [30-Min aiOS Blueprint](../setup-itself/30-min-aios-blueprint.md)). Just the basics: who you are, your company, your top 3 priorities.
- **Day 26-28 (15 min/day):** install Claude Code (Mac/Win/Linux). Move your workflow from Browser to Claude Code with CLAUDE.md.
- **Day 29-30 (off):** test the formalized version. Should feel 30-50% faster than ad-hoc.

### Week 6-7, Add a Skill (90 min total)

**Goal:** make the workflow autonomous, not prompt-by-prompt.

- **Day 31-35 (10 min/day):** browse the [Composio awesome-claude-skills](https://github.com/ComposioHQ/awesome-claude-skills) library. Find a skill that matches your use case.
- **Day 36-40 (10 min/day):** install + test the skill. Customize it for your context.
- **Day 41-49 (10 min/day):** use the skill daily. Track time savings.

### Week 8-9, Audit + Plan Use Case #2 (60 min total)

**Goal:** measure ROI, decide next step.

- **Day 50:** time audit. How much time per week did you save vs Week 1? Multiply by 4 weeks/month, then by your hourly value. That is the ROI of Use Case #1.
- **Day 51-55 (10 min/day):** if ROI is positive (>10x time invested), plan Use Case #2. Repeat the framework.
- **Day 56-60:** start Use Case #2 day-1 ritual.

## What you have at day 60

- ONE working AI workflow saving 4-8 hours/week (= 16-32 hours/month)
- A CLAUDE.md file that survives model updates
- One installed skill that runs autonomously
- Confidence to expand from here, knowing the muscle memory works

What you do NOT have (and that is fine):
- A full agent stack
- 20 skills
- MCP integrations
- Multi-agent orchestration

These come later. Foundation first.

## The 5 starter use cases, ranked by ROI

If you can not decide which use case to pick in Week 1, use this ranking:

| Use case | Avg time saved | Setup effort | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Email triage** | 8-12 hrs/week | Low | Anyone with 100+ emails/day |
| **Meeting summaries** | 4-6 hrs/week | Low | Anyone with 10+ meetings/week |
| **Daily brief** | 2-4 hrs/week | Medium | Anyone with multiple tools/contexts |
| **Status report writing** | 2-3 hrs/week | Medium | Founders with weekly board update |
| **Inbound message replies** | 3-5 hrs/week | Low | Anyone with high DM/support volume |

For most EO founders, **email triage wins** because it is universal and the pain is daily.

## What NOT to do in your first 60 days

- **Do not install 5 tools at once.** ChatGPT + Claude + Notion AI + Cursor + Gemini will overwhelm. One tool, one use case, period.
- **Do not subscribe to 5 newsletters.** Wait until day 60. Pace research is week 9, not week 1.
- **Do not try to learn prompt engineering theory.** Use Claude, get bad output, ask Claude how to improve the prompt. Faster than reading prompt-engineering books.
- **Do not build agents in week 4.** Agents are weeks 12-24, not weeks 1-8. Skills are the bridge.
- **Do not compare yourself to people who are 18 months ahead.** Your job is to be 60 days ahead of yourself. That is enough.

## When to upgrade from this plan

After 60 days + ONE working workflow, you can:
- Add Use Case #2 (repeat the 60-day framework, but compressed to 30 days, you know the rhythm)
- Add MCP integrations (see [MCP Cookbook](../integration-mcp/mcp-cookbook.md))
- Add a second skill (composio library is free)

After 6 months + 3 working workflows:
- Consider Claude Managed Agents for long-running tasks
- Consider team rollout (see [Team Rollout Playbook](../team-adoption/team-rollout-playbook.md))

After 12 months: you are no longer a beginner. You will have figured out what is right for you.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **„I have time to learn it all in one weekend."** No, you do not. You will burn out and abandon. 10 min/day beats 4 hours/Saturday.
- **„I should learn the technology before using it."** Wrong order. Use it. Theory follows practice for power users.
- **„I will switch tools when something better comes."** No. Stick with your tool for 60 days minimum. Switching costs are real.
- **„I need to learn prompt engineering first."** No. Just type what you would tell a smart intern. Prompt skill emerges from use.
- **„This use case is too small to bother with."** Email triage saving 30 min/day = 130 hours/year. That is „too small"?
