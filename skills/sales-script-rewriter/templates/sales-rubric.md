# Sales scoring rubric, defaults

> The 6-dimension scoring grid. Customize per your sales motion (B2B SaaS ≠ retail ≠ enterprise) and save to `memory/_meta/sales_rubric.md`.

---

## The 6 dimensions

### 1. Opening hook (first 30 seconds)

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | "Hi, this is X from Y, I wanted to talk about our product" |
| 2 | Names the prospect's industry but generic |
| 3 | Names the prospect's role pain ("I work with Heads of Sales who struggle with...") |
| 4 | Names the prospect's company-specific signal ("I saw your team is hiring 3 SDRs...") |
| 5 | Names the prospect's *yesterday* pain with evidence ("Your CEO posted on LinkedIn last week about...") |

### 2. Discovery quality

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Closed yes/no questions only ("Are you happy with your current solution?") |
| 2 | Open questions but generic ("Tell me about your business") |
| 3 | Open questions, surface-level ("What's your sales process?") |
| 4 | Open questions targeting decision dynamics ("Who else needs to bless this?") |
| 5 | Pattern interrupt + change-question ("Walk me through what changed in the last 90 days that made this a real conversation") |

### 3. Objection handling

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Rebuts the objection head-on ("That's not true, actually we're the cheapest") |
| 2 | Acknowledges and dismisses ("I hear you, but...") |
| 3 | Acknowledges + redirects ("That makes sense, what would you need to see to consider switching?") |
| 4 | Surfaces and validates the underlying concern ("So you're worried about migration risk, that's the right concern, here's how others handled it...") |
| 5 | Gets ahead of common objections preemptively ("You're probably thinking 'we already have a vendor', let me address that before you ask") |

### 4. Value framing

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Features ("We have AI, dashboards, and integrations") |
| 2 | Features + benefits ("Our AI saves you time") |
| 3 | Benefits in vague terms ("You'll be more productive") |
| 4 | Benefits in buyer's language with metric ("You'll cut your sales-prep time from 30 min to 5 min per call") |
| 5 | Buyer's outcomes in their KPI ("Your reps will hit quota faster, average is 2 weeks earlier ramp") |

### 5. Closing crispness

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | "Want to set something up?" or "Talk to you later" |
| 2 | Offers a vague next step ("Let me send you some info") |
| 3 | Offers 3+ meeting times to pick from |
| 4 | Offers 2 meeting times + "or what works for you" |
| 5 | Offers 2 + commitment ladder ("Tuesday 14:00 or Thursday 10:00, and would you bring your CFO since they'll need to weigh in?") |

### 6. Listen-to-talk discipline

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | < 30% buyer talk time, rep dominates |
| 2 | 30-40% buyer talk |
| 3 | 40-50% buyer talk |
| 4 | 50-60% buyer talk + uses pauses (no rushing to fill silence) |
| 5 | 50-60% buyer talk + names the silence ("I'll let that sit, what's coming up for you?") |

## Customization examples

### B2B SaaS (default above)
Standard 6-dimension grid as written.

### Enterprise (long sales cycles)
Add a 7th dimension: **Stakeholder mapping** (did the rep identify economic buyer + champion + technical evaluator?). Adjust closing scoring to "got the next meeting with the right people" instead of "got the next meeting".

### High-velocity inside sales
Tighten time targets: longest monologue <40s, talk ratio target 35/65, discovery questions ≥4 (not 6).

### Consultative / advisory sales
Add a "diagnostic depth" dimension. Closing crispness lowered in importance, relationships matter more than the scheduled meeting.

### Retail / B2C
Different rubric entirely. Opening hook becomes "rapport in 10 seconds". Discovery becomes "understanding what they want to feel". Closing becomes "ask for the sale clearly".

---

## How to calibrate

1. Score 5 calls manually using this rubric
2. Have 2 sales colleagues independently score the same 5 calls
3. Compare scores, gaps > 1 point on any dimension means your team has different definitions
4. Reach team agreement on definitions before deploying the skill
5. Re-test on 5 more calls
6. Once team agrees, save the calibrated rubric to `memory/_meta/sales_rubric.md`

Without team calibration, scores are vibes-based and reps will reject them.
